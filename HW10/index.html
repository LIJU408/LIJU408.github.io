How to define GDP?

According to this website, Gross domestic product (GDP) is the total monetary or market value of all the finished goods and services produced within a country’s borders in a specific time period.

Are you curious how USA GDP has changed over years?

The below scroller shows the important events for USA GDP since 1947.

Year 1950, GDP increase 8.7% Korea War

Year 1959, GDP increase 2.6% Recession Starts

Year 1966, GDP increase 6.6% Vietnam War

Year 1975, GDP decrease 0.2% Recession Ends

Year 1978, GDP increase 5.5% Fed’s 20% rate hike ended inflation

Year 1998, GDP increase 4.5% LTCM crisis

Year 2000, GDP increase 4.1% Tech bubble burst

Year 2008, GDP decrease 0.1% Financial Crisis

Description of Dataset

I got the GDP dataset from IS445 course website.

The dataset measures monthly GDP data of USA from 1947 to 2019. It includes two columns: Date and GDP.

My components in this idyll website helped me to better demonstrate dataset in two ways:

  • Give some background knowledge arond GDP, including GDP definition, big events happening in USA GDP history.
  • Display the GDP data in multiple ways, via table and chart.
